Product Cycle

How the best product companies build — Basecamp ships in fixed-appetite cycles with hard commitment gates. Google, Duolingo, and Spotify run continuous discovery in parallel with delivery, never letting the build track starve the learning track. Teams like Intercom and Linear treat every problem as a hypothesis before it becomes a task.

This framework synthesises those practices into one process. Same skeleton for a 30-minute bug fix and a 4-week feature — the depth of each stage scales with the work, not the ceremony.

Business Touchpoint

Soft sync with business

Business sees research findings before PM spends time shaping. Prevents misdirected shaping effort.

⏱ 5 min – 2 hrs

What this is — and isn't

Is: Direction check. "Here's what we learned. Here's where we're heading. Does this match what you need?"

Is not: Scope negotiation or solution review. Business can redirect problem framing. They cannot dictate the solution.

Tier depth

TierFormatDuration
T0Skip — PM + eng own it—
T1Slack message with summary + thumbs up/down5 min
T2Async doc / Loom — stakeholder comments30 min
T3Sync meeting if misaligned after async shareUp to 2 hrs

Output

  • Direction confirmed note — 2–3 sentences in research doc
  • Revised problem framing — if stakeholder redirects
  • Significant redirect → loop back to Stage 2

Rule

Business can say "you've misframed the problem."

Business cannot say "build it this way."

Solution direction stays with PM + eng.

Shaping vs scope lock — the line

Shaping (here): What are we building at a rough level? What flows exist? What's explicitly out? Rough timeline agreed.

Scope lock (Stage 4): High-fidelity designs, full task breakdown, committed end date. Happens after betting table commits the pitch.

Commit Gate

Betting table

The only place where work is committed. Pitches are accepted, deferred, or discarded. No changes after this gate.

⏱ skip – 1 hr

Tier depth

TierFormatDurationNo-go trigger
T0Skip — PM + eng lead approve async—If fix takes >4× estimate → reclassify to T1
T1Async Bet / Kill — PM + eng lead15 minIf scope >2× estimate → stop and re-pitch
T230 min sync — PM + eng lead + design lead30 minIf design reveals 2× scope → back to shaping
T3Full betting table — all deciders1 hrDefined explicitly in pitch — reviewed at week 3 scope check

Commitment rule

Once committed: scope locked. Team gets full cycle uninterrupted. Reprioritization → next cycle's betting table only.

Discard, don't backlog

Pitches not selected are archived. If a problem is real, someone re-pitches it. Problems that die here weren't important enough or weren't shaped well enough — both are useful signals.

Full betting table flow (T3 · 1 hr)

Capacity check (10 min) — Eng lead presents available person-weeks.

Pitch presentations (30 min) — Each PM presents in 10 min. No Q&A during presentation.

Deliberation (15 min) — Deciders discuss without pitch authors.

Decisions (5 min) — Bet / Hold / Kill.

Stage 4

Scope lock

High-fidelity designs + full task breakdown + committed end date. Happens after the betting table commits the pitch. This is where breadboards become real designs.

⏱ 1 day – 2 weeks

The ±10% rule

Minor scope adjustments during build are OK without returning to betting. Anything beyond ±10%, or any change to the core problem, requires a new pitch next cycle.

Tier depth

TierDesignScopingDuration
T0No design neededEng breaks into tasks in <30 minCovered in shaping

T2Hi-fi screens + edge casesFull task board, deps flagged1–3 days

Input

  • Committed pitch from betting table
  • Breadboards from Stage 3
  • Appetite + rough timeline
  • Open questions from pitch

Output + Artifacts

  • High-fidelity designs — all screens + edge cases
  • Scope map — tasks in Linear, each ≤1 day
  • Dependency map — what blocks what
  • Committed end date — locked here

Process (T2/T3)

Kickoff (2 hrs) — PM walks team through pitch. All clarifying questions asked now — not during build.

Designer produces hi-fi — Breadboards → full screens. Edge cases, empty states, error states. Reviewed by eng for feasibility before finalising.

Eng decomposes scope — All tasks mapped. Each ≤1 day. Dependencies flagged explicitly.

Eng lead commits date — Confirms delivery within appetite. If not: cut scope, not extend timeline.

Business Touchpoint

Timeline handoff

Committed date shared with business after Stage 4. Anything before this is a guess, not a commitment.

⏱ 5 min async – 30 min

Core principle

Only commit a date after eng lead has signed off on decomposed scope. If business asks earlier, give a range at betting table: "2–4 week cycle. Committed date within 3 days of cycle start."

Stage 5

Build & release

Team executes against committed scope. No new priorities enter. PM shields the team; stakeholders receive updates, not give direction.

⏱ 30 min – 3–4 weeks

Board states (T2/T3)

In dev
WIP: max 2 tasks per engineer.
In review
Code review + QA. Target: 24 hr max.
Ready to release
Staged + approved. Max 48 hr here.
Released
In production. KPI tracking on. → Stage 6.

Rhythm (T2/T3)

Monday async update — What shipped, in progress, blockers. PM acts on blockers same day.

Week 3 scope check — Behind? Cut scope, not extend timeline. PM + eng lead in 30 min.

No mid-cycle reprioritization — New requests logged for next betting table.

Stage 6

Measure impact

Did we move the KPI? Closes the loop. Verdict determines whether Stage 1 is re-entered or the problem is solved.

Solved

Partially solved

Not solved
No signal. Return to S1 — revisit problem definition.
Post-Build Buffer

Cool-down

After Stage 6, before the next betting table. Scales with cycle length — roughly 15–20% of cycle duration.

⏱ 2–3 days (T2) · 1 week (T3)

Valid work

  • Bug fixes cut from the cycle
  • Technical debt paydown
  • Refactoring + performance
  • Exploration + prototyping
  • Documentation + tooling

Not for

  • Features that didn't get bet on
  • Urgent stakeholder requests
  • Unscoped mini-sprints

PM responsibility: protect this time.

Parallel: Preparing the next betting table

PM reviews Stage 6 impact reports from current cycle

Confirms which shaped pitches (Stage 3 outputs) are ready to bet

Business alignment — stakeholders present new problems, informal triage

Betting table runs on last day of cool-down — team exits with next cycle committed
